Treatment of Mental Illness

Psychotherapy:

Psychotherapy provides the patient with the opportunity to talk about their thoughts, express their feelings, problems, and difficulties, and then learn effective ways to deal with them.

There are many types of psychotherapy that the doctor uses in the treatment of different mental illnesses, and the appropriate type is chosen according to each case separately. The duration of treatment for mental illnesses is usually several months until the results begin to be noticed.

Some common types of psychotherapy include:

  • Cognitive behavioral therapy: This therapy is useful in changing the pattern of thinking and negative behaviors, and can be used in the treatment of depression, anxiety disorders, schizophrenia, and other psychotic disorders.
  • Interactive psychotherapy: Interactive therapy focuses on solving social relationship problems because of their impact on the feelings and behaviors of the patient, and is useful in treating mental illnesses such as depression.
  • Dialectical behavioral therapy: This therapy helps teach individuals how to control their emotions and reactions, and is used in the treatment of many psychological problems, especially borderline personality disorder.

Treatment of Mental Illness with Medication:

Medications reduce symptoms of mental illness, improve mood, and enhance an individual's ability to perform daily tasks.

Choosing the right medication depends on the patient's symptoms and health condition, and it may be necessary to try more than one drug under the supervision of a doctor to reach the best medication to treat mental illness.

The main classes of drugs used in the treatment of mental disorders include:

  • Antidepressants: These drugs are used in the treatment of depression, and can also be used in the treatment of other mental illnesses, such as anxiety, phobia, obsessive-compulsive disorder, as well as some types of eating disorders.
  • Antipsychotics: Antipsychotics are usually prescribed to treat psychotic mental illnesses, such as schizophrenia and schizoaffective disorder, and may be used in some cases of depression and severe anxiety. They may also be used in treating bipolar disorder by reducing mania.
  • Mood stabilizers: Mood stabilizers are used in the treatment of bipolar mental illness for their role in relieving severe mood swings, reducing the frequency of major depression and manic episodes.
  • Anti-anxiety (tranquilizers): Sedatives work to treat anxiety and sleep problems.

It should be noted that these drugs may be effective in reducing the symptoms experienced by the patient, but they are not without side effects. In some cases, these effects may be severe, and medications may cause withdrawal symptoms when stopping them. Therefore, psychiatric drugs should only be used with a prescription, and discontinuing them suddenly without consulting a doctor should be avoided.

Treatment of Psychiatric Diseases in the Hospital:

Treatment of serious mental illness, especially in severe episodes, may require a short hospital stay for appropriate treatment. Hospitalization may be required in the following cases:

  • When there is a danger to the patient's life, such as attempts to self-harm or suicide.
  • When there is a threat to the lives of others.
  • The need for intensive treatment, support, and continuous monitoring.
